Our History

The Center for Excellence in Higher Education (CEHE) was established in 2006 by a small group of philanthropists who were concerned about the growing challenges facing higher education in America. Until 2012, CEHE operated as a public charity. It conducted a series of research programs and supported faculty interested in improving higher education at universities around the country and internationally. CEHE also engaged in several important research projects, acting in the background to help philanthropists who wanted to promote structural reform of higher education more generally.

In 2012, CEHE identified a unique opportunity. The sole stockholder of a group of colleges, including CollegeAmerica, Stevens-Henager College, and California College San Diego wanted to become a nonprofit institution. They shared much of the same vision for improving higher education as the founders of CEHE. The colleges embody many of the principles CEHE sought to promote in its efforts to improve higher education. It was a perfect match, and in 2012 the colleges were merged into CEHE.

Going forward, CEHE has continued to work to make improvements in delivery methods and content in higher education, but rather than focusing that work simply on advocating for change, CEHE, through its colleges, is actively involved in providing educational opportunities to the thousands of students it now serves.

Our Schools

Independence University (IU) offers fully online degrees in healthcare, technology, business, and graphic arts. IU partners with over 430 organizations nationwide to make sure students gain the knowledge and experience needed to launch their careers after graduation.

California College San Diego (CCSD) has academic offerings in healthcare, business and technology. Locations include San Diego, San Marcos and National City. CCSD emphasizes personalized student support and providing career-oriented degree programs.
